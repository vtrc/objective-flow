# Explore Options

Explore options before committing to a path.

## Hard Gate

Do not shape, plan, execute, or recommend a consequential commitment until credible options have been compared and the direction is selected or approved.

## Process

1. Start from the problem frame; revise it if new information changes the objective.
2. Ask one question at a time. Ask only what materially affects the choice.
3. Propose two or three distinct approaches. State the trade-off, fit, and recommended option for each.
4. Recommend one approach based on the objective, constraints, and success condition.
5. Ask for explicit approval when the direction is consequential, multi-step, irreversible, costly, or user-facing.
6. Hand off to `shape-approach` after approval, `choose-option` when criteria conflict, or `frame-objective` when the objective changes.

Keep options concrete and comparable. Do not create false variety by renaming the same approach.
