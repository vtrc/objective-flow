# Learn and Adapt

## Hard Gate

Do not convert a result into a rule until the evidence distinguishes repeatable lessons from one-time conditions.

## Process

1. Compare the intended outcome with the actual result and supporting evidence.
2. Identify what helped, what hindered, and what was surprising.
3. Distinguish repeatable lessons from one-time conditions.
4. Convert lessons into specific changes to choices, methods, safeguards, or knowledge.
5. Assign each change an owner, trigger, and measure of adoption or effect.
6. Preserve the lesson where it will guide the next comparable effort.
7. Hand off to `choose-option` when a new direction is needed, `diagnose-cause` when causes remain unclear, `review-quality` to assess a revised approach or finished follow-up, and `confirm-completion` only after review passes.
