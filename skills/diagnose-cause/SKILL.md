# Find the Cause

## Hard Gate

Do not choose corrective action before the cause is supported by evidence or the remaining uncertainty is explicit.

## Process

1. Define the gap between expected and actual results, its scope, and its impact.
2. Collect direct evidence; distinguish observations from interpretations.
3. Map the sequence, conditions, decisions, and dependencies that produced the result.
4. Identify candidate causes and test each against the evidence.
5. Confirm the earliest controllable cause or state what remains uncertain.
6. Choose corrective and preventive actions, owners, measures, and review dates.
7. Hand off to `review-quality` to assess the diagnosis and actions. Hand off to `choose-option` when remedies compete, `capture-learning` after outcomes are measured, and `confirm-completion` only after review passes.
